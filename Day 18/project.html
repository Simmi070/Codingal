Chips

Potato chips were famously invented in 1853 by chef George Crum at Moon's Lake House in Saratoga Springs, NY. Annoyed by a customer complaining that his fries were too thick, Crum sliced potatoes paper-thin, fried them until crisp, and salted them heavily. Initially known as "Saratoga Chips," they gained popularity and became a staple snack.

Section 2

Key Moments in Potato Chip History: 1853 Origin: George Crum (born George Speck) invented them as a crispy, salty alternative to thick, soggy fried potatoes. Early 1900s Mass Production: Entrepreneur William Tappenden began selling chips in grocery stores, initially out of barrels. Packaging Revolution: In the 1920s, Laura Scudder invented the first wax-paper bag, allowing chips to stay fresh and be transported. Flavoring Evolution: In the 1950s, Joe "Spud" Murphy, founder of Irish company Tayto, invented the first flavored crisps: Cheese & Onion and Salt & Vinegar.
